# Business ideation and validation studio

Use this recipe when the product is still an idea. The goal is to learn faster,
not to generate flattering business prose. The system should seek
counter-evidence and decide whether to build, change, or stop.

## Prerequisites

```text
Verify target market, customer/job hypothesis, evidence-quality rubric, source
policy, experiment budget, and decision owner.
```

## Discovery and Feynman prompts

```text
Read product notes, market constraints, research sources, Feynman loop guide,
Karpathy retention rules, and business-domain source material.
```

```text
Explain the customer, job-to-be-done, painful alternative, adoption trigger,
buyer, channel, and evidence needed to disprove the idea.
```

## KBD prompts

```text
/kbd-assess ideation-studio
Assess assumptions, target users, risks, counter-evidence, experiment budget,
success thresholds, and decision timing.
```

```text
/kbd-analyze ideation-studio
Analyze market sources, comparable products, contrary evidence, user segments,
pricing constraints, distribution channels, and legal/ethical constraints.
```

```text
/kbd-spec ideation-studio
Specify assumption ledger, falsification tests, experiment cards, scoring
rubric, decision journal, research source registry, and retention policy.
```

```text
/kbd-plan ideation-studio
Plan learning before build work: Feynman gaps, research, interview/smoke tests,
prototype threshold, critic review, and retention.
```

## Implementation and verification

```text
Build only the minimum prototype or research artifact needed to test the
highest-risk assumption. Do not expand product scope until the evidence threshold
is met.
```

```text
Run the selected experiment, collect evidence, score the result, update the
decision journal, and show what decision the evidence supports.
```

## Stop evidence

Stop for no falsifiable assumption, no counter-evidence, unsupported market
claims, unclear buyer, missing scoring rubric, or evidence that fails the build
threshold.
