## Why

The public site promises ten staged application-building recipes but exposes none of
them. Each scenario needs a complete prompt sequence that teaches users how to move
from learning and requirements through bounded implementation, independent
verification, recovery, and retained improvement evidence.

> Scope: ten complete, copyable scenario prompt packs and the shared recipe anatomy
> that makes their success and stop conditions observable.

## What Changes

- Publish complete staged recipes for: the full KnowMe hybrid application;
  Flutter-only mobile with Rust FFI; Tauri desktop with local inference; full-stack
  automation; multi-tenant SaaS; a Claude Desktop-style local agent client; a
  business ideation studio; a native Rust agent; source-built multi-cloud deployment;
  and a branded documentation portal.
- Require every recipe to include prerequisites, sources of truth, discovery,
  Feynman, KBD assess/analyze/spec/plan, research, bounded implementation,
  public-boundary verification, independent critic, reflection/retention, recovery,
  authority, budgets, expected artifacts, stop conditions, and harness variants.
- Encode TJ-ARCH-MOB-001 and the 40 Prometheus Base Rules in relevant recipes,
  including shared-Rust ownership, platform selection, clean-worktree discipline,
  public-boundary proof, and no self-certified completion.
- Provide producer and critic role classes derived from the model registry rather than
  hard-coded model endorsements.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

- `scenario-prompt-packs`: Ten complete, validated application-development recipes
  with copyable phase prompts, observable evidence, recovery, and termination rules.

### Modified Capabilities

None.

## Impact

- Replaces the current outline-only scenario source with first-class nested pages
  under `docs/prompting/scenarios/`.
- Consumes the foundation schemas/model routing and the harness/loop playbooks.
- Adds no application runtime code; recipes describe how generated projects are built
  and verified.
